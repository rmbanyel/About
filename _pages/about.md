I am an MLIS/ MA in Digital Humanities candidate at the University of Alberta. I hold an MA in Heritage Studies from the University of Zimbabwe and a specialized certificate in Audio-Visual Cataloging from the Institut national de l'audiovisuel (INA) in France. My research focuses on archival silences, community archives, and digital preservation.

I have over 8 years of experience as an Archivist. My professional foundation was built at the National Archives of Zimbabwe, where I served as a Public Archives and Research Archivist. I also worked as a Part-time Tutor Instructor at the University of Zimbabwe, where I mentored undergraduate students in archival and information management.

Currently, I balance my graduate research with my role as an Archivist at the Spruce Grove Archives. I am dedicated to bridging the gap between traditional archival standards and modern digital needs, exploring how metadata and description can be used to amplify voices that have traditionally been excluded from the historical record.
